Cities to Explore

Each with its own soul, story and skyline

01

Kraków

"The Royal Capital"

Poland's most beloved city, brimming with medieval grandeur. Europe's largest medieval market square is ringed by Gothic churches and the magnificent Cloth Hall. Wawel Royal Castle towers above the Vistula, while Kazimierz pulses with cafés, galleries and synagogues.

02

Gdańsk

"Pearl of the Baltic"

A Hanseatic treasure on the Baltic Sea, Gdańsk dazzles with soaring Gothic architecture and colourful gabled merchant houses. Explore amber jewellery workshops and the birthplace of the Solidarity movement that changed Europe forever.

Long LaneGolden GateCrane GateWesterplatteSopot Beach

Wrocław

"City of a Hundred Bridges"

Built on twelve islands crisscrossed by rivers, Wrocław enchants with an explosion of colour. The Market Square is flanked by pastel tenements of cobalt, tangerine and rose. Over 300 hidden bronze dwarfs await discovery across the city!

Rynek SquareCentennial HallCathedral IslandBronze DwarfsBotanical Garden

⛰️ Mountains
04

Zakopane

"Winter Capital of Poland"

Nestled beneath dramatic Tatra peaks, this mountain resort enchants year-round. Krupówki street teems with folk crafts and smoked oscypek cheese. Hike to the glacial Morskie Oko lake or take the cable car for panoramas stretching into Slovakia.

Morskie OkoKrupówki StreetKasprowy WierchGubałówka HillTatra Nat'l Park

🐐 History
05

Poznań

"Birthplace of Poland"

Poland's oldest city — Poznań's Renaissance Old Market Square is one of Europe's finest. Each noon, two mechanical goats emerge from the Town Hall clock to butt heads. Sample the famous Saint Martin croissants and visit the original Polish Cathedral.

Old Market SquareOstrów TumskiImperial CastleRogal CroissantMalta Lake
🏙️
🏙️ Capital
06

Warsaw

"The Phoenix City"

Poland's dynamic capital, rebuilt from rubble after WWII, blends its UNESCO Old Town with glass skyscrapers and a roaring cultural scene. Visit the Warsaw Uprising Museum, stroll Łazienki Park and discover world-class jazz clubs and galleries.

When to Visit

Every season brings a different magic to Poland

🌸
Spring
April – May
★★★★☆

Blooming parks, Easter traditions, mild temperatures. Perfect for city walks without summer crowds.

☀️
Summer
June – August
★★★★★

Warm and gloriously lively. Best for Tatra hiking, Baltic beaches and rooftop bar evenings.

🍂
Autumn
September – October
★★★★☆

Golden forests, fewer tourists, mushroom-picking season and crisp, clear air.

❄️
Winter
December – February
★★★☆☆

Magical Christmas markets in Kraków & Wrocław. Skiing and snowshoeing in Zakopane.

Must-Try Polish Food

Hearty, honest and utterly delicious

🥟
Pierogi

Poland's beloved stuffed dumplings — potato & cheese, minced meat, or sauerkraut. Order fried for extra indulgence.

🥣
Żurek

Sour rye soup with sausage and hard-boiled egg, often served inside a hollowed bread bowl. A winter warmer.

🥩
Bigos

Hunter's stew of sauerkraut, fresh cabbage and smoked meats simmered for hours. Poland's soul food.

🧀
Oscypek

Smoked sheep's cheese from the Tatras, grilled and served warm with cranberry jam. A Zakopane essential.

🥐
Rogal Świętomarciński

Poznań's iconic flaky croissant filled with white poppy seed paste. Sold by the kilo and utterly irresistible.

🍰
Sernik

Polish cheesecake made with twaróg curd cheese — denser, richer and creamier than any other version.

🍺
Polish Beer

Żywiec, Tyskie & Okocim — outstanding lagers found in every bar, incredibly affordable.

🥃
Żubrówka Vodka

Poland's finest vodka, infused with bison grass. Mix with apple juice for the classic "Tatanka" cocktail.

Practical Tips

Everything a British traveller needs to know before going

💷
Currency

Poland uses the Polish Złoty (PLN). Very affordable — a restaurant meal often costs just £5–10. Cards widely accepted.

✈️
Flights

Direct flights from London, Manchester & Edinburgh with LOT, Ryanair & Wizz Air. Around 2–2.5 hours flying time.

🚄
Getting Around

PKP Intercity trains connect all major cities quickly and cheaply. Book in advance at intercity.pl. City trams are excellent.

🗣️
Language

English widely spoken in hotels and restaurants, especially by younger Poles. A few Polish words go a very long way.

🛡️
Safety

Poland is one of Europe's safest countries. Standard travel precautions apply. GHIC cards accepted for emergency healthcare.

🌡️
Weather

Summers reach 20–30°C. Winters cold and snowy, especially in the mountains. Pack layers for spring and autumn.

🔌
Plugs & Power

Type C/E sockets, 230V. UK travellers need a standard European adaptor — cheap and easy to buy before departure.

📱
Mobile & Data

UK SIMs work in Poland — check your provider's roaming policy. Local SIM cards available cheaply at any newsagent.
